# Case Study: Phishing Attack on College Students

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords
, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trustworthy entity.

A typical phishing attack begins with the attacker sending a fake email, SMS, or message that appears to
come from a legitimate source, such as a bank, university, or popular website. The message often creates urgency,
such as “Your account will be blocked” or “You have won a prize.” It includes a malicious link that redirects the
victim to a fake website designed to look real. When the user enters their login credentials or financial details,
the attacker captures the information. In some cases, victims are also asked to share OTPs, leading to unauthorized transactions.

College students are common targets because they frequently use online platforms, may lack awareness, and are often
attracted by offers like scholarships, internships, or discounts.

The consequences can be severe, including financial loss, identity theft, unauthorized access to personal accounts,
and emotional stress. In some cases, stolen data may also be misused for further scams.

This highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with unknown links and verifying sources before sharing any sensitive information.
